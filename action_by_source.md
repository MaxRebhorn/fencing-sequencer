🗡️ CUT LISTS BY SOURCE
Henry Charles Angelo

(Infantry Sword Exercise, 1845)

Numbered Cuts:

Cut 1 – diagonal, right → left, to head
Cut 2 – diagonal, left → right, to head
Cut 3 – horizontal/diagonal to right side
Cut 4 – horizontal/diagonal to left side
Cut 5 – vertical downward (top of head)
Cut 6 – upward, right side
Cut 7 – upward, left side
John Musgrave Waite

(Lessons in Sabre, Singlestick)

Numbered Cuts (same structure as Angelo):

Cut 1 – right → left, head
Cut 2 – left → right, head
Cut 3 – right side
Cut 4 – left side
Cut 5 – vertical downward (crown)
Cut 6 – upward right
Cut 7 – upward left
Giuseppe Radaelli

(reconstructed via Barbasetti / later Italian sources)

Radaelli does not use numbers, but defines cuts by type + target.

Molinelli (circular cuts)
Molinello to head (right)
Molinello to head (left)
Molinello to flank (right)
Molinello to flank (left)
Molinello to face
Direct cuts (shortest line)
Direct cut to head (right)
Direct cut to head (left)
Direct cut to flank (right)
Direct cut to flank (left)
Other offensive actions
Thrust (to chest/face)
Luigi Barbasetti

(The Art of the Sabre and the Epee)

Barbasetti formalizes Radaellian structure:

Cuts
Cut to head (right)
Cut to head (left)
Cut to flank (right)
Cut to flank (left)
Cut to face
Variants
Circular cuts (molinelli)
Direct cuts
Alfred Hutton

(Cold Steel, The Swordsman)

No strict numbering; describes cuts by direction:

Cuts
Downward cut (head)
Downward diagonal right → left
Downward diagonal left → right
Horizontal cut (right → left)
Horizontal cut (left → right)
Rising cut (right)
Rising cut (left)
Additional (older influence)
Moulinet cuts (full circular)
Charles Roworth

(Art of Defence on Foot)

Numbered Cuts (broadsword lineage)
Cut 1 – head (right → left)
Cut 2 – head (left → right)
Cut 3 – right side
Cut 4 – left side
Cut 5 – head (vertical)
Cut 6 – leg or low outside
Cut 7 – leg or low inside
⚠️ Notes for application design
British lineage (Roworth → Angelo → Waite) = stable 1–7 system
Italian lineage (Radaelli / Barbasetti) = no fixed numbering, must encode:
target (head / flank / face)
type (direct / circular)
Hutton = hybrid → best treated as directional cuts, not canonical set

(Infantry Sword Exercise, 1845)

Prime – low inside line
Second – low outside line
Tierce – outside (mid/high)
Quarte – inside (mid/high)
Quinte – high (head protection)
Sixte – high inside
